↳ Why Do We Follow the News?

14 Aug 2015
Listen Queue Download (26.7 MB)

A couple months ago while listening to Hello Internet, professional YouTube explainer CGP Grey talked about his reasons for not following the news. He raised an interesting question that left a mark on me: is keeping up with the news as righteous, necessary, and morally imperative as we commonly believe?

This episode of Freakonomics Radio grazes that question, but mainly tackles the related question of why we actually follow the news, and if it is for the righteousness, necessity, and moral imperatives that we commonly assume. It's an interesting question, and this podcast does a great job exploring it from multiple perspectives while giving the listener lots to think about.

Original Description

There are all kinds of civics-class answers to that question. But how true are they? Could it be that we like to read about war, politics, and miscellaneous heartbreak simply because it's (gasp) entertaining?
