What Is This Thing?

03 Aug 2015

Welcome to Pod Pod Pod, a curated playlist of the week's best podcasts. I'm your podcast DJ, Jeffinitely Jeff.

Here's how this thing is gonna work: subscribe to Pod Pod Pod and every Monday, Wednesday, and Friday I'll post a great podcast that I've found in the past week and I think you should try listening to.

Why should you care what podcasts I recommend? Well, I'm a little obsessed with podcasts. Obsessed enough that my phone has autocorrected a correctly typed "well" into the name of a podcast app that shut down a year ago. Obsessed enough to organize my to-do lists by what type of podcast I can listen to in the background—to subscribe to over 600 podcasts and listen to about 100 to 200 episodes a week. I have some fairly niche interests, but I'm going to be keeping these recommendations pretty mainstream—I plan for all of these episodes to be "must listens" for basically anyone. I want Pod Pod Pod to serve both as a beginner's orientation to podcasts and a long-time podcast fan's transfusion of new and exciting shows.

Some say we're in the middle of a podcast Renaissance, and it's undeniable that there are more amazing shows out there than there ever have been, but finding and trying out new podcasts still kinda sucks. So what makes Pod Pod Pod any different from the lush landscape of podcast recommendation newsletters? Pod Pod Pod is a newsletter, a Twitter account, a link blog, but primarily and most importantly: you can subscribe to it in your favorite podcast app and have 3 new awesome podcast episodes automatically appear each week. Pod Pod Pod is a meta-podcast playlist, a playlist of the best episodes of other podcasts, which sneaks into your podcast app disguised as a podcast.

From time to time I'll include a brief introduction to an episode if I think it's important to set the stage, and on rare occasions I may include post-episode reactions or discussions for special episodes. I'll never modify or hijack downloads of others' podcasts, and am only going to be linking directly to the original audio files, so these pre- and post-episode thoughts will be delivered as separate audio files in the podcast and blog feed. If you have your podcast app set up to automatically play podcasts sequentially, it should be pretty seamless. But if you're using reverse-chronological sorting (aka newest first): first of all, you should be ashamed of yourself and your heathen ways, and, second of all, I have an alternative feed with pre- and post-episode positions swapped so pre- will still come before and post- after if you insist on your immoral life style. I don't think these pre- and post-show bits will be frequent, but I'll see how things go and what you like.

I'll have a comment thread for each episode here, so check that out if you want to chat about any of the episodes I've linked to.

I'm hoping to have Pod Pod Pod in iTunes soon, but until then you can subscribe in a podcast app by copy and pasting the URL for the RSS feed (or the chrono-flipped RSS feed). If you're into Twitter, you can follow @podpodpodcast to keep up with the latest recommendations—or my personal account, @jeffinitelyjeff, if you want random musings and rants about software development, podcasting, and anime. If you want to fight back against Inbox Zero, subscribe to the recommendations as a weekly email newsletter.

This is a weird experiment, but I'm excited to share great audio with you. I hope you enjoy it.
